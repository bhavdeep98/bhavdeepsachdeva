Recently, I've been reflecting on a powerful statement by Dr. Andrew Ng: "I am not important, what I want to do is." This resonated deeply with me as I navigated a period of anxiety and uncertainty. It reminded me to focus on my goals and contributions, rather than getting caught up in self-doubt.

Inspired by this, I'm excited to share my research and thoughts here. This space is dedicated to exploring the fascinating intersection of:

  • Ethical AI: Building AI systems that are ethically sound from their inception.
  • Collaborative Multi-Agent Systems: Designing frameworks for productive interactions between humans and AI agents.
  • AI Safety and Human Psychology: Investigating the psychological impacts of AI on human trust, reliance, and well-being.

I believe that by combining human "slow thinking" with AI's "fast thinking," we can achieve extraordinary outcomes. But this requires careful consideration of the ethical implications and the potential impacts on human psychology.

My hope for this space is to:

  • Spark valuable conversations about the future of AI.
  • Contribute to the development of AI that truly benefits humanity.

Beyond my research, I'll also be sharing some personal reflections here. I believe that our values and experiences shape who we are, both as researchers and individuals.
